tasks This article explains how to use the help desk system.

Using the Help System

The help system allows you to view information about the tasks that you can perform on the screen you are viewing and any problems that you may encounter while performing these tasks.

Viewing an Article

When you open the help window, the help articles for the screen will be listed in the Tasks section and Problems section. To view an article, click on the name of the article. The content of the article will appear in the help window.

Throughout the article there may be a number of hyperlinks and Locator icons. When you click a hyperlink, you will be taken to the definition of that term. When you click a locator icon, the help pointer will show you where the field is located on the screen.

To return to the list of articles, click the Home button.

Using the Picker

The picker allows you to find information about a field on the screen. To use the picker, click the Picker button and click the field on the screen.

If there is no information for the field you have selected, a message will appear in the help window. Select another field or click the Cancel button.

Viewing the Glossary of Terms

To view the glossary of terms for the screen, click the Glossary button. The terms will appear in the help window in alphabetical order.

View the General Help Articles

To view the general help articles, click the General Help button. The articles will appear in the help window. You can view these articles from any screen within Squiz CMS.

Viewing the Articles for Another Part of the System

To view the articles for another part of the system, click the Menu button and select the system that you want to view. The articles for that system will appear in the help window.

When you view an article for another system, a blue box may appear at the top of the article. This box outlines the steps you need to perform in order to view the correct screen for the system. If you do not perform these steps, you may not be able to understand the article.
